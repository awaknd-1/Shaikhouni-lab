Focus Areas

What We Study

Our interdisciplinary team applies cutting-edge computational and clinical methods to understand and treat pediatric epilepsy.

🧠

Pediatric Epilepsy

Investigating the electrophysiological signatures of Dravet Syndrome and other childhood epilepsies using scalp and intracranial EEG.

📊

EEG Signal Processing

Developing automated pipelines for EEG segmentation, artifact rejection, and sleep staging using machine learning approaches.

🌙

Sleep Neuroscience

Characterizing sleep architecture in pediatric epilepsy, including spindle dynamics and slow-wave activity, to uncover biomarkers of disease severity.

⚡

Neural Circuit Dynamics

Mapping functional connectivity and network-level changes in epileptic brains to guide surgical and neuromodulation therapies.

🤖

Machine Learning in Neurology

Building deep learning models for automated seizure detection, classification, and outcome prediction from EEG and clinical data.

🏥

Clinical Translation

Bridging bench to bedside — translating computational discoveries into actionable clinical tools and improved surgical outcomes.

About the Lab

Advancing Care for Children with Epilepsy

Led by Dr. Ammar Shaikhouni, MD, PhD, our lab is embedded within the Department of Neurosurgery at Nationwide Children's Hospital — one of the nation's leading pediatric academic medical centers.

We combine neurosurgical expertise with rigorous computational analysis to study how abnormal neural activity emerges, propagates, and can be interrupted in children with pharmacoresistant epilepsy.
