Research

Exploring the neural basis of pediatric epilepsy through computation and clinical neuroscience.

Overview

Our Research Mission

The Shaikhouni Lab combines clinical neurosurgical expertise with rigorous computational analysis to understand how abnormal neural activity arises and propagates in pediatric epilepsy. By developing novel algorithms and leveraging multi-site EEG datasets, we aim to identify reliable biomarkers and inform better surgical and therapeutic outcomes for children with treatment-resistant seizures.

Projects

Current Research Programs

🧬

Dravet Syndrome: Electrophysiological Phenotyping

Dravet Syndrome is a severe, treatment-resistant epileptic encephalopathy typically caused by SCN1A mutations. We are building the first large-scale multi-site EEG cohort to characterize the electrophysiological phenotype across disease stages, leveraging data from Nationwide Children's Hospital and Children's Hospital Los Angeles.

  • Automated EEG preprocessing and epoch-level quality control
  • Unsupervised clustering of sleep and wake EEG phenotypes
  • Longitudinal tracking of EEG signatures with anti-seizure medication changes
  • Correlation of EEG features with cognitive and behavioral outcomes
🌙

Sleep Architecture as a Biomarker in Pediatric Epilepsy

Disrupted sleep is a hallmark of many childhood epilepsies, yet quantitative sleep biomarkers remain underutilized clinically. We develop and validate automated sleep-staging pipelines using YASA and deep learning, then apply them to characterize sleep macro- and micro-architecture — including NREM spindle density, slow-wave activity, and REM dynamics — in Dravet patients compared to controls.

  • Automated sleep staging (N1/N2/N3/REM/Wake) from clinical EEG
  • NREM sleep spindle detection and morphological analysis
  • Sleep slow-oscillation and spindle coupling in epileptic networks
  • Sleep metrics as predictors of treatment response
🤖

Machine Learning for EEG Segmentation & Seizure Detection

Manual annotation of pediatric EEG is time-intensive and subject to inter-rater variability. We are building automated pipelines for epoch segmentation, artifact detection, and ictal event classification using supervised and self-supervised learning approaches validated against expert clinical annotations.

  • Transformer-based EEG foundation models for seizure detection
  • Self-supervised pre-training on unlabeled pediatric EEG archives
  • Cross-patient and cross-site generalization benchmarks
  • Clinical integration and real-time alerting systems
⚡

Neural Circuit Mapping for Epilepsy Surgery

For children with drug-resistant focal epilepsy, surgical resection or neuromodulation offers the best chance of seizure freedom. We use intracranial EEG and functional connectivity analysis to map epileptogenic networks preoperatively and evaluate surgical outcomes.

  • Interictal and ictal network analysis with SEEG/ECoG
  • Effective connectivity and directed information flow
  • Outcome prediction from network biomarkers
  • Neuronavigation integration in the OR
Tools & Methods

Our Toolkit

We develop open-source software and leverage best-in-class frameworks.

MNE-Python

EEG/MEG preprocessing, source estimation, and time-frequency analysis.

YASA

Automated sleep staging and spindle/slow-wave detection from polysomnographic signals.

PyTorch / scikit-learn

Deep learning models and classical machine learning for EEG classification tasks.

Python / Pandas

Data wrangling, statistical analysis, and reproducible research pipelines.

Jupyter Notebooks

Interactive analysis, visualization, and reproducible reporting for all projects.

GitHub

Version-controlled, collaboratively developed code shared openly with the research community.
